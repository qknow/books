INTRODUCTION

Food Preservation

Food Preservation is the method used to prevent food from going bad or from micro-organisms growing on it.

There are reasons for preserving foods. They are;

a. It increases food availability. This is because food will not go bad.

b. To make certain foods available even after their seasons have passed. Food that is cultivated have seasons in which they grow, some only grow twice a year. Food preservation helps preserve those foods throughout the year.

c. Some foods are harvested in large quantities they cannot be consumed within that short period so they are preserved for future use.

d. Some farms are far from homes or markets so food harvested from those farms must be preserved before taking them to their destinations.

e. It prevents farmers from massive losses of harvest.

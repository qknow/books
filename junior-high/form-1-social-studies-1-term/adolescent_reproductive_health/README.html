Adolescent Reproductive Health

Adolescence

It is the transitional stage from childhood to adulthood where physical and psychological development takes place. It takes place from ages 10 through 19. Adolescence can be split into two (2) phases; Early Adolescence (10years to 14years) and Late Adolescence (15years to 19years).

Since adolescence is a transition from one stage to another certain physical changes occur within the male and female body, these mark the transition occurring.


Physical changes in Boys;

  1. Sexual/reproductive organs increase in size (penis and testicles)
  2. Development of facial hair (beard, moustache) as well as pubic hair (around genitals) and hair under the armpits.
  3. Boys experience a break in their voices; the voice becomes deeper due to the expansion of the voice box.
  4. Boys also develop more pronounced muscles and broader shoulders as compared to girls; they also become heavier than girls and grow taller.
  5. Boys start to have sexual urges or desires for the other sex and the need to have sex with them.
  6. The testicles start to produce sperms and they can impregnate girls through sexual intercourse. They experience “wet dreams” at night and ejaculate which sometimes may be embarrassing for them. This is also known as Nocturnal Emissions. 7.


Physical changes in Girls;

  1. The breast develops and enlarge during this period, they also develop glands which produce milk for breastfeeding when they give birth.
  2. Growth of pubic hair around the vagina and hair grows in the armpits.
  3. Their hips begin to broaden and waist also takes a smaller rounder shape as they develop a more feminine physique. Broadening of hips allows for females to carry babies in their wombs.
  4. Menstruation occurs; where the ovaries begin to produce eggs and the ovulation cycle begins with discharge of blood and tissue from the vagina when the eggs are not fertilized.
  5. They also develop an attraction for the opposite sex.

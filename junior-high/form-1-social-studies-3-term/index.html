Land Use

Land use is referred to as the modification and conversion of natural environment or in some cases wild lands to suit human living conditions. Through these modifications man develops settlements, fields and pastures.

wild lands: these are the wild natural areas which are not modified by human activity.


A settlement is described as a place where people live. Each type of settlement may vary in size or population. Some forms of settlements are hamlets, villages, towns and cities.

Settlements include houses, roads, hospitals, markets, religious places of worship (churches, mosques, temples etc)

Fields and pastures are cultivated on and farm animals are reared. In most countries Fields and Pastures are found in the rural areas where population is sparse.


Land use is closely linked with city planning (what is the land used for?). Governments make sure to monitor land use planning in order to regulate it and avoid any conflicts of land use that might occur like overcrowding. The shift of population from rural to urban areas made it important for governments to try and control housing, industrialization and trade within urban areas.


The government uses various regulations or methods to implement their land use plans, a commonly known one is zoning.
